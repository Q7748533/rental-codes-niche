Avis Corporate Codes 2025: Save Up To 30% on Los Angeles Rentals

My LAX Avis Rental Strategy

Renting a car at LAX right now is brutal with those crazy California taxes and airport facility charges, but I've found a solid workaround. Last month, I flew into Los Angeles for a quick weekend trip. The standard rate for a basic mid-size SUV was completely out of control—nearly $100 a day before taxes. I wasn't about to pay retail, so I dug into my Flyertalk notes to test a few Avis Worldwide Discount (AWD) codes.

The Heavy Hitter: Corporate AWD

The absolute best rate I pulled recently was using the Deloitte corporate code (AWD A075100). It immediately knocked about 30% off the base rate, and often includes loss damage waiver coverage. But here's the reality check for LAX: While the Avis Preferred booth in the parking garage is usually pretty chill, the main counter agents have been getting surprisingly strict in 2025. I’ve had friends get asked to show a corporate email address, an intranet login, or a physical employee badge twice this year. If you use a heavy-hitting business code, you need to be prepared to back it up or risk getting bumped to the expensive walk-up rate.

The Safest Bet

If you don't want to sweat bullets walking up to the rental counter, your Safest Bet is the official Costco member code (AWD A108326) or the standard AAA discount (AWD A177232). Sure, you might only save 10% to 15% off the base rate, but it almost always includes a free additional driver. More importantly, flashing a Costco or AAA card is completely stress-free and 100% bulletproof.

Insider Tip for LAX

Insider Tip: Never book an Avis rental at LAX without signing up for Avis Preferred first. It's totally free. When you land, you can bypass that massive, soul-crushing line of exhausted tourists, hop on the shuttle, and walk straight to your designated spot in the garage—your keys are already inside. Also, make sure to link your American Airlines AAdvantage account to your Avis profile; they are currently throwing out some massive bonus miles for rentals originating in Southern California!

💡 Insider Tip: Book directly on the rental company's website and avoid third-party sites like Expedia or Kayak. I've seen $50+ in surprise fees added at check-in for third-party bookings. Corporate rate bookings earn full loyalty points and qualify for free promotions.
