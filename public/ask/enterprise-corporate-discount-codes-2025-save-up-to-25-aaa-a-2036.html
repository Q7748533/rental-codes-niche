The Truth About Enterprise and AAA in 2025

Renting a car at LAX or MIA lately can be brutal on the wallet, and I see folks on Flyertalk constantly asking about the Enterprise AAA discount. Here is the insider truth: Enterprise actually quietly ended their official national AAA partnership a while back (Hertz scooped that up). But don't panic. I found a workaround that gets you the same, if not better, rates without needing that little plastic card.

The Heavy Hitter: Big Corporate Code

If you want to see your daily rate plummet, the Ford Motor Company corporate code (XZ23236) is a legendary money-saver. I've used this to shave up to 25% off last-minute SUVs. However, a massive warning from my own experience: Enterprise has gotten surprisingly strict with ID checks in 2025, especially at major hubs like JFK. If you use a big automotive or tech code, the desk agent will almost certainly ask for a corporate badge or an email from a company domain. If you don't have it, they'll bump you to the walk-up rate, which is financial suicide.

The Safest Bet: Alumni & Association Codes

Since the AAA route is dead at Enterprise, my go-to "safe" code is the State of New York / SUNY Alumni code (XZ41148). It reliably knocks 10% to 15% off the standard retail rate. The beauty of this code is that it's technically an alumni and leisure discount. I've rolled through dozens of Enterprise locations with this, and they never ask for proof—because who carries around their college alumni card anyway?

My Insider Tip

  • Status Match for the Skip: Enterprise is notorious for long counter lines. Take your hotel status (like Marriott Gold), match it to National Executive, and then status match that over to Enterprise Plus. It gets you upgraded and lets you use the priority VIP line.
  • Dodge the Fees: If you're flying into a major hub, take a cheap Uber to a neighborhood Enterprise branch. The base rate with these codes will be similar, but you'll completely avoid the 20%+ airport concession fees!

💡 Insider Tip: Book directly on the rental company's website and avoid third-party sites like Expedia or Kayak. I've seen $50+ in surprise fees added at check-in for third-party bookings. Corporate rate bookings earn full loyalty points and qualify for free promotions.
