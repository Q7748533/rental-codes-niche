Beating the Rental Car Racket in 2026

Let's be real—renting a car at major hubs like SFO or MIA can be absolutely brutal on your wallet these days. But after years of living out of my suitcase and lurking on Flyertalk, I've found a few workarounds. If you're tied to IBM, you're sitting on a goldmine for Hertz rentals.

The Holy Grail: IBM's Hertz CDP

The legendary IBM Corporate Discount Program (CDP) code is 40000. In my experience, applying this code is like magic; I've seen it slash base rates by up to 40%, especially on last-minute weekday bookings. It often includes loss damage waiver (LDW) for business trips and caps those ridiculous daily rates.

But here is the catch: Hertz has cracked down hard. If you're picking up from the Ultimate Choice lot at LAX or JFK, expect the gate agent to play interrogator. They are strictly enforcing ID checks in 2026. You will need a physical IBM badge, a recent pay stub, or be able to show an email from an active @ibm.com address. If you're just borrowing the code and get caught, they'll bump you to the walk-up rate, which will completely ruin your travel budget.

The Safest Bet for Everyone Else

If you forgot your badge or don't actually work for Big Blue, don't panic. My go-to safe code is the AAA CDP (1805452) or the Delta SkyMiles CDP (165385). You might only save 10% to 20%, but nobody is going to grill you at the exit gate. Just show your AAA card or Delta app if asked, and you're golden.

My Insider Tip

Here is a trick I use all the time: Make sure you add the CDP to your Hertz Gold Plus Rewards profile before you book. By doing this, you skip the counter entirely and go straight to your car in the Gold lot. While gate agents still do random audits, bypassing the main desk drastically reduces your chances of getting hawked for corporate ID. Plus, you'll still earn those sweet Hertz points for your next personal vacation.

💡 Insider Tip: Book directly on the rental company's website and avoid third-party sites like Expedia or Kayak. I've seen $50+ in surprise fees added at check-in for third-party bookings. Corporate rate bookings earn full loyalty points and qualify for free promotions.
